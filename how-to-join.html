How to Join CNYmesh

CNYmesh is an open, community-driven mesh networking project in Central New York. There are no membership fees, no sign-up forms, and no gatekeeping — just connect, participate, and help the network grow.

Who we are

CNYmesh is a grassroots group of volunteers building a resilient, community-owned radio mesh network across the Syracuse region. Our focus counties are Onondaga, Oswego, Madison, Oneida, Cayuga, and Cortland, with ongoing coordination toward Rochester, Buffalo, Ithaca, and Albany.

We operate networks using both Meshtastic and MeshCore — two different open-source protocols that run on affordable LoRa radios. You don't need to be an engineer or a licensed ham operator to participate. Most people are up and running within an afternoon.

What participation looks like

There's no single way to be part of CNYmesh. Members contribute in different ways depending on their interests and resources:

  • Carry a node — a phone-sized device extends the network wherever you go
  • Host a fixed node — place a node at home, work, or a high-elevation site for 24/7 coverage
  • Join the conversation — share knowledge, ask questions, and help onboard others
  • Test and document — field tests, range experiments, and real-world notes help everyone
  • Build infrastructure — high-elevation relay nodes extend coverage for an entire neighborhood

Getting started with a node

If you're ready to put a device on the air, the first step is picking a protocol. Both Meshtastic and MeshCore are active on the CNYmesh network — the Mesh Protocols page can help you decide which to try first.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a ham license?

No. Both Meshtastic and MeshCore operate on the 915 MHz ISM band, which is license-free under FCC Part 15. You can participate without any radio license at all.

How much does it cost?

Most starter devices cost between $30 and $100. There are no ongoing fees or subscriptions involved in CNYmesh.

Is there a formal membership process?

No. CNYmesh is an open community. Showing up in Discord or Facebook and putting a node on the air is all it takes.

What range can I expect?

  • Urban / dense area: ½–2 miles
  • Suburban / rural with good placement: 5–10+ miles
  • High elevation or line-of-sight: 20+ miles is possible

Will it work during a power outage?

Yes. Nodes communicate peer-to-peer by radio. Battery-powered or solar-powered nodes continue operating when the grid is down.
