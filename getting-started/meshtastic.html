Getting Started with Meshtastic

Everything you need to get a Meshtastic node on the air and connected to CNYmesh — from picking hardware to configuring channels and joining the community.

Step 1 — Choose your hardware

Meshtastic runs on affordable LoRa radio boards. Most devices cost between $30–$100 and require no soldering. Popular choices used within CNYmesh include:

  • LILYGO T-Beam — built-in GPS, good all-around starter node
  • Heltec V3 — compact, battery-friendly, easy to deploy indoors
  • LILYGO T-Echo — low power with e-ink display, great for portable use
  • RAK WisBlock — modular platform, popular for fixed infrastructure installs

Check the Meshtastic hardware page for a full device list and firmware compatibility.

Step 2 — Flash firmware (if needed)

New devices may ship with Meshtastic pre-installed. If not, use the Meshtastic web flasher to install the latest stable firmware. Connect the device via USB and follow the on-screen steps — no special tools required.

Step 3 — Install the Meshtastic app

Download the official Meshtastic app for your phone:

  • Android: available on Google Play
  • iOS: available on the App Store

Open the app, pair to your device over Bluetooth, and set your node name or callsign. This is how you'll appear to others on the mesh.

Step 4 — Join the CNYmesh channels

CNYmesh uses a set of shared channels to organize communication across Central New York. Scan the QR code below from within the Meshtastic app to automatically load the full channel configuration.

CNYmesh Channel Structure

Channel Name Role Purpose
LongFast Primary Node discovery and general chat; compatible with the wider Meshtastic default channel
CNYmesh Secondary Primary internal channel for CNYmesh community traffic
CNYmesh-EAS Secondary Local weather alerts and emergency notices for Central New York

How to scan the QR code

  1. Open the Meshtastic app and go to the Channels screen
  2. Tap the QR scan icon and point your camera at the code below
  3. Select Replace when prompted
  4. Tap Accept to save the configuration

Step 5 — Configure LoRa radio settings

For your node to communicate reliably with others on the CNYmesh network, your LoRa settings need to match our standard configuration.

LoRa Configuration

  • Modem Preset Long_Fast
  • Region United States
  • Hop Limit 3–7
  • TX Enabled True
  • TX Power 30
  • Override Frequency 906.875 MHz
  • Frequency Slot 20
  • Override Duty Cycle True
  • OK to MQTT True

Step 6 — Configure MQTT (optional but recommended)

Enabling MQTT bridges your node to the broader internet-connected mesh and feeds the CNYmesh live dashboard. This is especially useful for fixed infrastructure nodes.

MQTT Configuration

  • MQTT Server mqtt.meshtastic.org
  • Root Topic msh/US/NY/CNY
  • Uplink Enabled True (on CNYmesh channel)
  • Downlink Enabled True (on CNYmesh channel)

The CNY root topic keeps our regional traffic organized and separate from other areas.

Step 7 — Put your node on the air

Once configured, your node will start participating in the mesh automatically. A few tips for getting the most out of your deployment:

  • Place near a window or outdoors — elevation makes a big difference
  • Power from AC or solar for around-the-clock operation
  • Weather-proof outdoor enclosures are important for CNY winters
  • Check the coverage map to see where gaps exist and where you can help
