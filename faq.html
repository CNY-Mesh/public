Frequently Asked Questions

What is CNYmesh?

A grassroots effort to build a community-powered Meshtastic network across Central New York using LoRa radios.

Do I need a ham license?

No for 915 MHz ISM (license-free). Ham use on other bands requires proper licensing/configuration.

How much does it cost?

Most starter devices cost $30–$100.

What range can I expect?

  • Urban: ~½–2 miles
  • Suburban/rural with good placement: 5–10+ miles
  • High elevation/line-of-sight: 20+ miles possible

Will it work during an outage?

Yes. Nodes communicate directly by radio. With battery/solar, they can stay up during extended outages.

Is it secure?

Messages can be encrypted. It’s community tech—not a replacement for enterprise-grade secure comms.

Where is coverage focused?

Onondaga, Oswego, Madison, Oneida, Cayuga, and Cortland counties—plus bridges to Rochester, Buffalo, Ithaca, and Albany.

How can I help?

  • Host a node (home, business, high-elevation)
  • Share RF knowledge (hams welcome)
  • Donate gear or assist with installs
  • Join mapping and field tests

How do I connect with the community?
